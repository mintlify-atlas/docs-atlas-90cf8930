My Auto Guide features an intelligent maintenance tracking system that calculates when your vehicle needs service based on the last maintenance date. The system uses circular progress indicators with color-coded alerts to keep you informed at a glance.

## Maintenance Types

The app tracks five critical maintenance items, each with its own service interval:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Chain Lubrication" icon="link">
    **Every 15 days** - Essential for motorcycle chain maintenance
  </Card>
  <Card title="Air Filter" icon="filter">
    **Every 90 days** - Keep your engine breathing clean air
  </Card>
  <Card title="Oil Change" icon="oil-can">
    **Every 25 days** - Regular oil changes extend engine life
  </Card>
  <Card title="SOAT (Insurance)" icon="shield-halved">
    **Every 365 days** - Mandatory vehicle insurance
  </Card>
  <Card title="Technomechanical Inspection" icon="car-burst">
    **Every 365 days** - Required safety inspection
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Visual Indicators

Each maintenance item displays a **circular progress indicator** showing the percentage of service life remaining:

### Color Coding System

- 🟢 **Green (&gt;50%)**: Maintenance is current, no action needed
- 🟡 **Yellow (25-50%)**: Service due soon, plan accordingly
- 🔴 **Red (&lt;25%)**: Service overdue, immediate attention required
- 🔴 **Red Pulsing**: Maintenance deadline has passed

<Info>
The color-coded system lets you assess your vehicle's maintenance status instantly without reading detailed information.
</Info>

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Set Last Maintenance Dates">
    Navigate to "Parametrización de mantenimientos" from the Tools section on your vehicle dashboard.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Dates for Each Service">
    Use the date picker to enter when each maintenance item was last completed:
    - Tap the "Elegir" (Choose) button next to each maintenance type
    - Select the date from the calendar picker
    - Repeat for all maintenance items you want to track
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save and Calculate">
    Tap "Guardar y calcular" (Save and Calculate) to update your maintenance schedule. The app will:
    - Calculate remaining service life as a percentage
    - Determine if any maintenance is overdue
    - Schedule local notifications for upcoming deadlines
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Monitor Your Dashboard">
    Return to the main dashboard where circular indicators now display:
    - Current percentage for each maintenance item
    - Color-coded status (green/yellow/red)
    - Visual alerts for items requiring attention
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Smart Notifications

The app automatically schedules **local notifications** when maintenance is due:

- Notifications appear even when the app is closed
- Alerts are sent on the exact due date for each service
- If maintenance is already overdue, you receive an immediate notification
- Each maintenance type has its own notification channel

<Tip>
Set all your maintenance dates at once when you first register your vehicle to get the most accurate tracking from day one.
</Tip>

## Calculation Formula

The app uses a straightforward calculation:

```
Days Elapsed = Current Date - Last Maintenance Date
Percentage Remaining = 1 - (Days Elapsed / Service Interval)
```

For example, if you changed oil 10 days ago (25-day interval):
- Days Elapsed: 10
- Percentage Remaining: 1 - (10/25) = 60%
- Status: Green ✅

<Note>
The app stores maintenance dates in the `vehiculos` table with fields like `last_cadena`, `last_filtro`, `last_aceite`, `last_soat`, and `last_tecno`.
</Note>

## Overdue Maintenance Alerts

When you save maintenance dates, if any item is already overdue, you'll see:

- A red snackbar notification at the bottom of the screen
- Message: "Hay mantenimientos vencidos, realizar cuanto antes" (Overdue maintenance detected, complete as soon as possible)
- The indicator will pulse red on the dashboard
- An immediate push notification reminding you to service your vehicle

<Warning>
Never ignore red indicators - overdue maintenance can lead to vehicle damage, safety issues, or regulatory problems.
</Warning>
